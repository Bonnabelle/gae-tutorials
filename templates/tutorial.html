Are you tired of getting error after error when trying to host your GAE app on appspot? Isn't it frustrating when someone says "just hit the deploy button" for the hundredth time? How about when you look over Stack Overflow for an answer but are still confused? And don't forget how helpful the documentation can be sometimes!

I'm here to clear the air and provide a simple tutorial on how to host your successfuly deploy your GAE apps. Firstly, choose your language below. (Java coming soon)




  1. First off, go to Google Cloud and sign in to your google account. This will be the developer account for your project, so choose wisely!

  2. Second, hit the button at the top right-hand corner that says "Console".

  3. Now hit the little tab that says "Project" right next to the big "Google Cloud Platform" text on the top left corner of the screen.

  4. Hit "Create Project." For this example I named mine memed-tutorial.



    5. IMPORTANT: Make sure you give your project THE SAME NAME as your GAE application. You can also edit the project id, right underneath the project name. The project id will be the url followed by .appspot.com when you're finished.


  5. Now you should be back on the homepage, and your current project is the one you just created. Look at the bottom right corner of the homepage and you should see something like this:



  6. Click on the arrow and you should see this:



  7. Copy the project ID. Now we need to install Google SDK tools for our console.

  8. Go to Google Cloud's SDK download page and follow this tutorial.

    9. IMPORTANT: MAKE SURE TO CHECK THE OPTION TO INSTALL BETA TOOLS AS WELL, YOU WILL NEED THESE!

  9. Now that that's done, open up Google App Engine and your console!

  10. Now cd into your project folder and when you get there, type $gcloud --project=[insert project name here] beta app create (no dollar sign, of course). The --project is a flag that specifies which project you are deploying. If this is your first time deploying an app, it won't really matter if you put it there, but if you've been deploying a few, this is an easy way to ensure your site is being deployed for a specific project.



  11. You should get a lovely little stacktrace and then you're presented with a choice:



  12. Choose your timezone. You should then get another stacktrace.

  13. If your code "exited with code 0", congratulations! It worked! If not, retrace your steps and maybe try again. (Or google it. like I had to.)

  14. Now comes the easy part! Click your project in Google App Engine, and finally hit that Deploy button! It may prompt you to sign in to access developer tools and that's okay; just sign in with the same account you used earlier for Google Cloud Platform. Afterwards, you should get a stacktrace of Google doing its magic.



  15. If it exited with code 0...


  16. Your website is up and running at [project-id].appspot.com!!

    17. I guess you could say... we are number one.

Remember, when you need to update your site, just hit deploy again. Thanks for reading, hope this helps! If you get stuck anywhere just contact me on Slack. (Unless you're a total stranger. In that case, please google it.)
